The Art of Betta Breeding

Step 1: Selecting Your Breeders

Choose healthy, vibrant males and females with desirable traits like fin shape, color intensity, and scale patterns. Look for compatibility in size and temperament to ensure successful pairing.

Tip: Quarantine new fish for 2-4 weeks to prevent disease transmission.

Step 2: Conditioning for Breeding

Feed high-protein live foods (brine shrimp, daphnia) for 1-2 weeks. Maintain water at 78-82°F with pristine quality. Introduce the pair gradually using a chimney or divider to build interest without aggression.

Tip: Females show vertical bars when ready; males build bubble nests.

Step 3: The Spawning Process

Remove the divider and supervise. The male wraps around the female to fertilize eggs. Collect eggs in the bubble nest. Remove the female post-spawn to avoid injury; male guards the nest.

Tip: Dim lighting reduces stress; expect 100-500 eggs per spawn.

Step 4: Raising Fry

Eggs hatch in 24-48 hours. Feed infusoria or micro-worms initially, transitioning to baby brine shrimp. Perform small water changes daily. Separate fry at 4-6 weeks to prevent fighting.

Tip: Use aged water; monitor for deformities to cull early for quality lines.

Step 5: Understanding Genetics

Track traits like dominant/recessive colors (e.g., red dominant over blue). Experiment with line breeding for stability or outcrossing for vigor. Document pedigrees for consistent results.

Tip: Start with simple crosses; advanced tools like Punnett squares help predict outcomes.
